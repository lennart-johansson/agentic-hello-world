Ode to the Giraffe

Tall against the amber sky,
You reach where other creatures shy,
With neck like a tower, proud and fine,
You touch the tops of the acacia line.

Your coat is a map of golden brown,
A patchwork robe, a living crown,
You stride across the open plain,
Where wind and silence softly reign.

No creature stands as tall as you,
No eyes see more of morning's dew,
You drink the sun before the rest,
And prove that height is nature's best.
